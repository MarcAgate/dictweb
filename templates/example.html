བྱང་ཆུབ་སེམས་དཔའ་ ILL General «Bodhisatva». Translation of the Sanskrit «bodhisatva». See བོདྷ for important notes on the spelling of the term. Even though most Western Buddhists spell the term «bodhisattva» in fact all earlier Tibetan texts spelled the term as given. The great Dilgo Khyentse Rinpoche when asked about this stated that bodhisatva and vajrasatva, too, were the correct spellings and that there was a difference in meaning between «satva» and «sattva». The difference is that the latter refers more to a person in general, a being, whereas the former refers to someone who is an heroic type of person, a warrior, someone with great courage and commitment. The Tibetans translated the Sanskrit literally: «bodhi» or བྱང་ཆུབ་ in Tibetan refers to the enlightenment of a buddha and «satva» or སེམས་དཔའ་ in Tibetan refers to someone who has a mind that is heroically committed, in this case to the enlightenment of a buddha.## The term bodhisatva is unique to the ཐེག་པ་ཆེན་པོ་ Great Vehicle. It is used to distinguish the practitioner of the Great Vehicle from the practitioners of the ཐེག་པ་དམན་པ་ Lesser Vehicle. Practitioners of the Lesser Vehicle have a motivation which is centred on removing themselves from the unsatisfactoriness of cyclic existence and becoming an arhat for their own salvation. Practitioners of the great vehicle have a motivation of becoming a buddha so that they can remove all sentient beings from the unsatisfactoriness of cyclic existence and bring all of them to perfect buddhahood. The lack of selfish concern and the willingness to undertake a much more difficult and long journey for the sake of others is the hallmark of a practitioner of the great vehicle which results in them being called «hero of enlightenment».## The distinguishing feature of a bodhisatva is that they have aroused བྱང་ཆུབ་ཀྱི་སེམས་ «bodhicitta», the mind which is bent on full enlightenment for the sake of all sentient beings. 586677
